Passive Voice — загальна форма і вживання

Правило to be + V3, коли вживаємо пасив

GENERAL FORM OF PASSIVE VOICE
Subject + to be + Ved / V3 + by + agent
WE USE PASSIVE VOICE
when it’s not important who or what is performing the action, but the action itself is important:
e.g.: Active: They produce most gadgets in Asia. (we don’t know who exactly produces them)
Passive: Most gadgets are produced in Asia.

when we don’t know who is a performer of the action:
e.g.: Active: Someone stole my sister’s bike yesterday. (we don’t know who stole it)
Passive: My sister’s bike was stolen yesterday.
USE IT RIGHT!
To show who did the action, we use 'by':
e.g.: This house was built by my grandfather.
ACTIVE VOICE PASSIVE VOICE
Present simple
+ + V / Vs + + am / is / are + Ved/V3
- + don’t / doesn’t + V - + am / is / are+ not + Ved/V3
? Do / does ++ V? ? Am / Is / Are ++ Ved/V3?
e.g.: People buy a lot of cars every year. e.g.: A lot of cars are bought every year.
Past Simple
+ + Ved / V2 + + was / were + Ved / V3
- + didn’t +V - + was / were + not + Ved / V3
? Did ++ V? ? Was / Were ++ Ved / V3?
e.g.: She bought this cake yesterday. e.g.: This cake was bought yesterday.
