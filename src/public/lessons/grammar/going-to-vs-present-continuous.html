Наміри, розклади та домовленості

GOING TO PRESENT CONTINUOUS
(future meaning)
+ I + am + going to + V
He/She/It + is + going to + V
You/We/They + are + going to + V
e.g.: I’m going to enter the university next year. 		I + am + Ving
He/She/It + is + Ving
You/We/They + are + Ving
e.g.: I’m going to the concert of Rihanna tonight.
- I + am not + going to + V
He/She/It + isn’t + going to + V
You/We/They + aren’t + going to + V
e.g.: We aren’t going to buy the tickets to that concert. 		I + am not + Ving
He/She/It + isn’t + Ving
You/We/They + aren’t + Ving
e.g.: We aren’t flying to Munich tomorrow.

  • To describe plans, intentions and ambitions we have for future:
e.g.: I’m going to fly to Florida next year. I should buy tickets soon.
(It’s just a plan)
  • To describe an arrangement or appointment in the future (there is nearly always a time expression:
e.g.: I’m flying to Florida in April. I’m glad that I bought the tickets five months ago.
(I’ve arranged everything)

USE IT RIGHT!
When we use go after be going to we can omit it:
We are going to (go to) London this year.
