Future — will, may/might та прислівники ймовірності

Прогнози, ступені впевненості, порядок слів

WILL + INFINITIVE
(prediction) 		MAY / MIGHT + INFINITIVE
(possibility)
e.g.: I think we will stay at home.
He is sure that Rosa will text him.
I hope that I’ll see you soon. 		e.g.: We might go camping tomorrow, but it depends on the weather.
I may see him at work, but I'm not sure.
Use will / won't + infinitive for future predictions.
We often use I think / I don't think ...+ will +infinitive

e.g.: I think he'll win the game.
I don't think he'll win the game.
NOT I think he won't win. 		Use might / may and might not / may not + infinitive to talk about a future possibility

e.g.: It might / may be true. = It’s possible that it will be true.
Do you remember the form?
Very certain
  • Computers will definitely / certainly be more powerful.
Likely
  • Accommodation will probably be more available in the future.
Possible
  • It is possible that electricity will be cheaper in the future.
  • Electricity may / might be cheaper in the future.
  • Perhaps electricity will be cheaper in the future.
Unlikely
  • IPhones probably won’t be much different in the future.
Very unlikely
  • Petrol definitely / certainly won’t be cheaper in the future.
What is the position of adverbs?
+ Computers will definitely / certainly be more powerful.
- Petrol definitely / certainly won’t be cheaper in the future.
Pronunciation
Contractions in positive and negative sentences, but not in questions:
will = ‘ll ___________ will not = won’t
e.g.: Will we stay here? No, I think we’ll leave. / No, we won’t, it’s better to leave.
