Small talk і обговорення

Питання, вітання, думки, згода/незгода

Asking personal questions to catch up with a speaker

You’re not from our office, are you?
Which company do you work for?
What have you been up to lately?
Are you still working at Belvedere Inc.?
How long have you had that job?
How have you been?
How’s your family?
Have you tried the chocolate cake?

Greeting each other

Nice to meet you.
Long time no see.
Fancy seeing you here.

Finding out more information about others

How do you know Mary then?
Who’s that girl over there?
How do you two know each other?
Could you introduce me to her?

Agreeing

  • I totally agree.
  • I couldn't agree (with you) more.
  • Me neither (to agree with a negative statement).
  • No doubt about it. / Absolutely.
  • You're absolutely right.

Expressing an opinion

  • Personally, I think ... / I suppose ...
  • To be honest, ... / It seems to me that...
  • If you want my honest opinion, ...
  • As far as I’m concerned...
  • If you ask me,...

Asking for opinions

  • Do you agree? / Don't you think ...?
  • What do you think? / What are your thoughts on ...?
  • Where do you stand on ...? / How do you feel about that?

Disagreeing

  • I’m afraid I disagree with you. / I totally disagree.
  • That's true, but... / You have a good point, but...
  • I see what you mean, but...
  • You can’t be serious!

Likes / Dislikes

  • I truly love watching thrillers.
  • I’m fond of listening to funk.
  • I like learning languages.
  • I’m keen on doing yoga.

Cause and result

  • I want to be healthier, that’s why I eat raw food.
  • It’s healthy, because of the vitamins. / because I consume vitamins.
  • I don’t drink milk due to lactose intolerance, so I choose a dairy‑free option.

Certainty vs possibility

  • It will definitely / certainly happen.
  • It will probably be fine.
  • It may / might / could be a problem.
