In subject questions the question word IS the subject, so we can’t put an auxiliary / modal verb before the subject. That’s why we use the word order of a regular sentence.

Pay attention:
Who / What is always third person singular, even if we know that the answer is plural.

For example:
Who knows the answer? - We all know the answer.
What has happened? - The cats have had a fight.

Use it right!
When telling a joke or story, we use present simple tense to show the event in a dynamic manner.
