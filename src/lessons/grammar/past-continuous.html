PAST SIMPLE PAST CONTINUOUS
+ to be
I / She / He / It + was
You / We / They + were
e.g.: We were very busy yesterday.

other verbs
+ Ved/V2
e.g.: She finished work at 8 p.m. 		I / She / He / It + was + Ving
You / We / They + were + Ving
e.g.: Sarah was playing the piano when the guests knocked on the door.
- to be
I / She / He / It + wasn’t
You / We / They + weren’t
e.g.: He wasn’t at home at 5.

other verbs
+ didn’t + V
e.g.: She didn’t go to that concert yesterday. 		I / She / He / It + wasn’t + Ving
You / We / They + weren’t + Ving
e.g.: We weren’t working at 6 o’clock yesterday.
? to be
Was + I / she / he / it?
Were + you / we / they?
e.g.: Where were you last night?

other verbs
Did ++ V?
e.g.: Did you call me 5 minutes ago? 		Was + I / she / he / it + Ving?
Were + you / we / they + Ving?
e.g.: What were you doing when your brother was playing Alias?
  1. Completed actions in the past
    e.g.: We finished school in 2007.
  2. Past states
    e.g.: Mike was very sad yesterday.
  3. Actions happened one after another
    e.g.: He came in, took off his coat and sat down.
  1. Two actions which were in progress at the same time
    e.g.: While she was cooking lasagna, he was playing Dungeons and Dragons with his friends.
  2. An action, event or situation that was in progress at specific time in the past.
    e.g.: We were watching “The Big Bang Theory” at 6 o’clock yesterday.
  3. A past action in progress, which was interrupted by another past action
    e.g.: When I was having breakfast, my mother phoned me.
