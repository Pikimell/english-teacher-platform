Describing how you look now

He’s got that ridiculous moustache.
He looks like Homer Simpson.
He looks terrible / so handsome / so creepy.
Joanne looks gorgeous.

Describing changes

He used to be the hottest guy in school.
She used to be so quiet and shy.
We used to tease her. Didn’t she use to wear glasses?

Doctor

  • What’s the matter?
  • How long have you had it / this problem?
  • Where does it hurt?
  • Can I have a look at ...?
  • I’ll give you some medicine / pills / antibiotics.
  • It’s nothing to worry about.

Patient

  • My (knee) hurts.
  • I’ve got a stomachache / a sore throat.
  • I’ve had it for two days.
  • I can’t workout / walk / sleep.
  • It hurts when I walk / press it.
  • I feel terrible / sick / cold.

Describing your problem

I can’t live without coffee. My boyfriend is addicted to social media.

Asking for help

What can I do about it? Can you help him? What would you do?

Helping someone

My advice would be to seek professional help. You should try talking to him...

How to order your instructions

Firstly / Then / Next / After that / Finally ...

How to tell what is necessary

You need to ... / It’s important to ... / Don’t ... / You should/shouldn’t ...
